# Greek Theologically Rich Terms Register — New Testament

**Purpose:** Lock default renderings for key Greek theological terms across all 27 NT books. Same word + same type of context = same rendering. Departures must be documented in `translator_notes`. This register is the NT parallel to the Hebrew Default Term Register in the Multi-Agent Consistency Rules.

---

## How to Use This Register

1. **Start with the default rendering.** If the context fits the default, use it. Do not invent variation for variety's sake.
2. **Vary when the Greek context genuinely demands it.** Greek words have real semantic ranges — honor that. But variation must be motivated by the text, not by stylistic restlessness.
3. **Document every departure.** When you use a rendering other than the default, add a `translator_notes` entry explaining why. Example: "Here pistis describes the reliability of God's promise — 'faithfulness' rather than 'faith,' since the subject is God's character, not human belief."
4. **The `key_terms` `rendered_as` field must match what you actually used** in the `rendering` text of that verse. Do not put the default if you used something different.
5. **Cross-Testament Link** connects to the Hebrew register. When a Greek term translates or echoes a Hebrew concept, the link ensures both testaments render the underlying idea consistently.

|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|

| σῴζω | sōzō | **save** | When the context is physical healing, "heal" or "make well" is acceptable (e.g., Mark 5:34 "Your faith has made you well") | Hebrew yasha' — the root behind yeshu'ah |

| ἁμαρτία | hamartia | **sin** | When the collective/abstract sense is primary (Rom 5-8), retain "sin" as a singular noun — do not pluralize unless the Greek is plural (ἁμαρτίαι) | Hebrew chatta't — sin/sin offering |

### Faith / Righteousness

| Greek | Transliteration | Default Rendering | When to Vary | Cross-Testament Link |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| πίστις | pistis | **faith** | When the subject is God or Christ (e.g., Rom 3:3, the faithfulness of God), use "faithfulness." When the sense is "pledge" or "proof" (e.g., Acts 17:31), use "assurance" — document each departure | Hebrew emunah — faithfulness, reliability, trust |
| δικαιοσύνη | dikaiosynē | **righteousness** | When the forensic/legal sense is primary (Rom 3:21-26), retain "righteousness" — do not substitute "justice" unless the context is explicitly about social justice (e.g., some OT quotations) | Hebrew tsedeq/tsedaqah — righteousness in the Hebrew register |
| δικαιόω | dikaioō | **justify** | In contexts where the meaning is clearly "declare righteous" (Paul's forensic usage), "justify" is the default. In James 2:21-25, where the meaning is "vindicate" or "show to be righteous," document the nuance in translator_notes | Hebrew tsadaq (Hiphil) — to declare righteous |
| χάρις | charis | **grace** | When the sense is "thanks" (e.g., Luke 6:32-34, "what credit is that to you?"), vary accordingly. When the sense is "favor" in a social context (Luke 2:52), "favor" is acceptable | Hebrew chen — grace, favor |

### God / Christ / Spirit

| Greek | Transliteration | Default Rendering | When to Vary | Cross-Testament Link |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| λόγος | logos | **Word** (capitalized when referring to Christ) | Lowercase "word" when referring to speech, message, or teaching (e.g., "the word of God" as Scripture/message). In John 1:1-14, always capitalized as a Christological title | Hebrew davar — word, matter, thing. The Johannine Logos draws on both Hebrew davar and Greek philosophical usage |
| Χριστός | Christos | **Christ** | When the titular sense "the Anointed One" is foregrounded (e.g., John 1:41, 4:25), use "Messiah" and note the Greek-Hebrew connection. When Christos functions as a proper name (Paul's letters), use "Christ" | Hebrew mashiach — anointed / anointed one (see Hebrew register) |
| κύριος | kyrios | **Lord** | When quoting OT passages where the Hebrew has YHWH, use "LORD" (small caps / all caps) to preserve the Tetragrammaton distinction. When kyrios means "sir" or "master" in ordinary address, use "sir" or "master" — document the decision | Hebrew adon (lord/master) and YHWH (when the LXX uses kyrios to translate the divine name) |
| πνεῦμα | pneuma | **Spirit** (capitalized for the Holy Spirit) | Lowercase "spirit" for human spirit, unclean spirits, or the general sense of "breath/wind" (John 3:8 plays on wind/Spirit — document the wordplay in translator_notes) | Hebrew ruach — spirit, wind, breath (see Hebrew register: ruach YHWH, ruach Elohim) |
| δόξα | doxa | **glory** | When the sense is "opinion" or "reputation" (rare in NT theological usage), vary accordingly | Hebrew kavod — glory, weight, honor (see Hebrew register) |
| παράκλητος | paraklētos | **Advocate** | In John 14-16 (the Farewell Discourse), always "Advocate." In 1 John 2:1, also "Advocate." Do not transliterate as "Paraclete" in the rendering text — transliteration may appear in translator_notes for explanation | No direct Hebrew equivalent; the role parallels the OT intercessor/mediator concept |

### Eschatology

| Greek | Transliteration | Default Rendering | When to Vary | Cross-Testament Link |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| παρουσία | parousia | **coming** | Do not transliterate as "Parousia" in the rendering text. When the sense is simply "presence" (Phil 2:12), use "presence" | No direct Hebrew equivalent; the concept of the Day of the LORD (yom YHWH) is the OT parallel |
| βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ | basileia tou theou | **kingdom of God** | In Matthew, where the text reads βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν (basileia tōn ouranōn), render as "kingdom of heaven" — do not harmonize Matthew to the other Gospels | Hebrew malkut — kingdom, kingship, reign |
| αἰώνιος | aiōnios | **eternal** | The olam/aiōnios debate is real: both Hebrew olam and Greek aiōnios can mean "age-long" rather than "infinite." Default to "eternal" but document passages where "of the age" or "age-long" is a defensible alternative in translator_notes | Hebrew olam — perpetuity, long duration, eternity |
| ἀνάστασις | anastasis | **resurrection** | Rarely varies. When used metaphorically (e.g., spiritual renewal), note the metaphorical extension | No direct Hebrew equivalent; the concept develops from Dan 12:2 and Isa 26:19 |
| ἔσχατος | eschatos | **last** | In the phrase "last days" (ἐσχάταις ἡμέραις), render as "last days" — do not use "end times" or "final days" | Hebrew acharit (ha-yamim) — the latter days |

### Other Critical Terms

| Greek | Transliteration | Default Rendering | When to Vary | Cross-Testament Link |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| σάρξ | sarx | **flesh** | Do NOT render as "sinful nature." The Greek means flesh — physical, mortal, human. Paul's theological loading of the term is real, but the rendering must preserve the Greek word, not impose an interpretation. Explain Paul's usage in translator_notes | Hebrew basar — flesh |
| μυστήριον | mystērion | **mystery** | This is a revealed secret, not an unsolved puzzle. Do not use "secret" as the default — "mystery" captures the technical Pauline sense of something once hidden but now disclosed | Hebrew raz (Dan 2:18-19, Aramaic) — secret, mystery |
| πληρόω | plēroō | **fulfill** | In Matthew's fulfillment quotations (1:22, 2:15, 2:17, etc.), always "fulfill." When the sense is "fill" or "complete" (e.g., Phil 2:2 "complete my joy"), vary accordingly | Hebrew male' — to fill, fulfill |
| μετάνοια | metanoia | **repentance** | Do not render as "change of mind" — the NT term carries the full weight of turning back to God, not merely intellectual revision | Hebrew teshuvah (from shuv) — turning, return, repentance |
| ἀρνίον | arnion | **Lamb** (capitalized in Revelation when referring to Christ) | In Revelation, always capitalized as a Christological title. Outside Revelation (John 21:15, "Feed my lambs"), lowercase | Hebrew seh/kebes — lamb; the Passover lamb (seh ha-pesach) of Exodus 12 |
| μαρτυρία | martyria | **testimony** | When the emphasis is on the person bearing witness rather than the content of what is said, "witness" is acceptable | Hebrew edut — testimony |
| σταυρός | stauros | **cross** | Rarely varies. Do not substitute "stake" or "execution pole" — "cross" is the established rendering and the Greek term's standard meaning in Roman-era usage | No direct Hebrew equivalent |
| ἀλήθεια | alētheia | **truth** | Rarely varies. In John's usage (John 14:6, "I am the way, the truth, and the life"), always "truth" | Hebrew emet — truth, faithfulness, reliability |
| σῶμα | sōma | **body** | When Paul uses sōma for the corporate body of Christ (1 Cor 12, Rom 12), retain "body" — the metaphor is deliberate | Hebrew geviyyah/basar — body/flesh |
| δοῦλος | doulos | **servant** | The literal meaning is "slave," and in contexts where the social institution of slavery is in view (Philemon, 1 Cor 7:21-23), "slave" is the accurate rendering. | αἷμα | haima | **blood** | When referring to Christ's atoning death ("the blood of Christ"), always "blood" — do not substitute "death" or "sacrifice" | Hebrew dam — blood |
| πρόσωπον | prosōpon | **face** | When the sense is "person" or "presence" (as in 2 Cor 2:10), vary accordingly | Hebrew panim — face, presence |

## Locked NT Formulas

Certain phrases recur across NT books and must be rendered identically each time they appear. These are the NT equivalent of the OT regnal formulas.

### Pauline Letter Openings

**Grace-and-peace formula:**

> Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

This formula appears in Romans, 1 Corinthians, 2 Corinthians, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, 2 Thessalonians, and Philemon. Render it identically each time. Minor variations in the Greek (e.g., 1 Thessalonians lacks "and the Lord Jesus Christ" in some manuscripts; Colossians has "God our Father" without "and the Lord Jesus Christ" in some texts) should follow the critical text and be noted in translator_notes.

### Pauline Letter Closings

**Grace benediction:**

> The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.

Variations include "with you all," "with your spirit," or expanded forms (2 Cor 13:14, the Trinitarian benediction). Render the core formula consistently and document expansions in translator_notes.

**Trinitarian benediction (2 Cor 13:14):**

> The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all.

This is the only full Trinitarian benediction. Render it exactly as above.

### Revelation: Letters to the Seven Churches

**Opening formula (Rev 2-3):**

> To the angel of the church in [city] write:

Render identically for all seven churches. Follow with: "These are the words of..." (introducing each Christological title).

**Hearing formula (Rev 2-3):**

> Whoever has ears, let them hear what the Spirit says to the churches.

Render identically in all seven letters.

**Overcoming formula (Rev 2-3):**

> To the one who overcomes...

Render identically in all seven letters. Do not vary between "conquers," "is victorious," and "overcomes."

### Gospel Passion Narrative Terms

The following terms must be rendered consistently across all four Gospels in the trial, crucifixion, and resurrection accounts:

| Greek | Context | Locked Rendering |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| σταυρόω (stauroō) | Crucifixion | **crucify** — not "execute" or "put to death on a cross" |
| ἐγείρω (egeirō) | Resurrection | **raise** — "He was raised" (divine passive) |
| ἀνίστημι (anistēmi) | Resurrection | **rise** — "He rose" (active) |
| μνημεῖον (mnēmeion) | Burial/Easter | **tomb** — not "grave" or "sepulcher" |
| σινδών (sindōn) | Burial | **linen cloth** — not "shroud" |
| συνέδριον (synedrion) | Trial | **Sanhedrin** — transliterated, not "council" |
| πραιτώριον (praitōrion) | Trial | **Praetorium** — transliterated |
| κεντυρίων / ἑκατόνταρχος (kentyriōn / hekatontarchos) | Crucifixion | **centurion** — not "officer" or "captain" |

### Beatitudes Formula (Matthew 5:3-12)

> Blessed are the [group], for they [promise].

Use "Blessed" — not "Happy" or "Fortunate." The formula must be consistent across all Beatitudes.

### Lord's Prayer (Matthew 6:9-13 / Luke 11:2-4)

Render Matthew's longer form and Luke's shorter form from the Greek, not from liturgical tradition. Differences between Matthew and Luke must be preserved — do not harmonize. Document departures from familiar English renderings in translator_notes.

### Johannine "I Am" Statements (John's Gospel)

> I am the [metaphor].

The emphatic ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi) must be rendered "I am" in all seven metaphorical statements (bread of life, light of the world, gate, good shepherd, resurrection and the life, way/truth/life, true vine) and in the absolute uses (John 8:58, "Before Abraham was, I am").

## Cross-Testament Consistency Summary

The following table maps the most important concept-pairs across testaments. When rendering NT passages that echo OT concepts, ensure the English captures the continuity:

| Concept | Hebrew Term (OT) | OT Default | Greek Term (NT) | NT Default | Consistency Note |
|---------|------------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Covenant | berit | covenant | diathēkē | covenant | Never "testament" in either testament |
| Faithful love | chesed | faithful love | agapē (covenantal) | love | The NT does not have a single Greek equivalent of chesed; agapē carries part of the weight, charis carries another part |
| Redemption | ga'al / go'el | redeem / kinsman-redeemer | lytrōsis / apolytrōsis | redemption | The kinship dimension of go'el is not present in the Greek; note this when the NT echoes OT redemption language |
| Righteousness | tsedeq / tsedaqah | righteousness | dikaiosynē | righteousness | Same English rendering in both testaments |
| Holy | qadosh | holy | hagios | holy | Same English rendering in both testaments |
| Glory | kavod | glory | doxa | glory | Same English rendering in both testaments |
| Peace | shalom | peace | eirēnē | peace | Same English rendering; eirēnē in greetings echoes shalom |
| Spirit | ruach | Spirit / spirit | pneuma | Spirit / spirit | Same capitalization rules in both testaments |
| Anointed | mashiach | anointed / anointed one | Christos | Christ / Messiah | When Christos carries the titular force, "Messiah" makes the link explicit |
| Law | torah | law / instruction | nomos | law | When torah means "instruction" broadly, the OT rendering may differ from the NT "law" — note this |
| Truth | emet | truth | alētheia | truth | Same English rendering in both testaments |
| Repentance | shuv / teshuvah | turn / return | metanoia | repentance | The Hebrew emphasizes turning; the Greek emphasizes the change. Note this distinction in translator_notes when both concepts are in play |
